TASK
Evaluate whether a Canadian education program (CIP) is a realistic ENTRY PATHWAY into a Canadian occupation (NOC).

ENTRY PATHWAY = the CIP prepares someone to qualify for or reasonably pursue entry into the occupation described by the NOC with normal post-graduation steps (e.g., licensing, residency, apprenticeship, or certification that the CIP is intended to lead into).

The goal is not skill similarity — it is career pathway alignment.

OUTPUT LABELS
Return exactly ONE label:

YES     → the CIP is an intended or typical pathway into the occupation
UNSURE  → the provided text is insufficient or ambiguous to decide
NO      → the CIP prepares for a different profession (not a real pathway)

You must not invent new labels.

---

PRIMARY DECISION RULE (most important)
Base your decision mainly on NOC employment_requirements.

If the CIP does not logically lead into the requirements described there, the answer is NO.

main_duties are supporting evidence, not the primary determinant.

---

HARD RULES

1) Credential-gated occupations
If employment_requirements mention licensing, certification, registration, regulated profession, board exam, or apprenticeship:

- The CIP must clearly represent preparation for that profession/pathway based on the provided CIP text.
- If not → NO

Do not assume a degree implies a licence unless the CIP text indicates it.

---

2) Domain mismatch
If the professions belong to clearly different domains (examples):
- policing vs carpentry
- business vs healthcare practitioner
- engineering vs artist
- telecommunications vs financial advising
- medical residency vs mechanical trade

→ NO even if some skills overlap.

Generic transferable skills NEVER justify YES.

---

3) Adjacent fields
If the CIP is in a related academic/professional field and could reasonably lead someone toward that occupation with typical next steps → YES.

Examples:
engineering → engineer occupation
nursing → nursing occupation
accounting → accounting occupation
law → lawyer

If unclear → UNSURE.

---

CONFIDENCE (0.00–1.00)

Confidence represents how certain you are that your chosen label is correct.

It is NOT tied to the label category and must not be chosen to fit a range.

Interpretation:
0.95 → almost certainly correct
0.80 → very likely correct
0.60 → probably correct but notable uncertainty
0.50 → genuinely uncertain
0.30 → probably incorrect
0.10 → almost certainly incorrect

Choose any value that reflects the strength of evidence in the provided text.
Two similar cases should rarely have identical confidence values.

---

RATIONALE REQUIREMENTS
1–2 short sentences.
Must reference:
• one concrete CIP detail AND
• one NOC employment requirement or duty

Do not justify using generic skills.

---

OUTPUT FORMAT
Return ONLY JSON with keys:
label, confidence, rationale, model_version, prompt_version

model_version: short string
prompt_version: "{{prompt_version}}"
